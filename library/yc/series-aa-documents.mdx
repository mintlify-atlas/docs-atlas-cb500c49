## What is Series AA?

The **Y Combinator Series AA** is a set of standardized legal documents for equity financing rounds, specifically designed for YC-funded startups raising capital from angels and early-stage investors, typically in the $250K-$2M range.

<Note>
Series AA is a lightweight preferred equity financing structure with simple terms that are generally balanced but lean toward being founder-friendly. The "AA" designation distinguishes these seed-stage documents from later "Series A" venture capital rounds.
</Note>

## Purpose & Philosophy

Y Combinator created Series AA documents to make equity financings:

  <Card title="Faster" icon="bolt">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Simpler" icon="feather">

  <Card title="Founder-Friendly" icon="handshake">

## When to Use Series AA

Series AA documents are appropriate when:

<Check>**You've converted SAFEs** and are ready for a priced equity round</Check>
<Check>**Angels want equity** instead of convertible instruments</Check>
<Check>**You have product-market fit** and recurring revenue</Check>
<Check>**Round size is $250K-$2M** from multiple angel investors</Check>

<Warning>
For larger rounds ($2M+) or institutional VC involvement, you may need more comprehensive documents like NVCA or Series Seed templates.
</Warning>

## Document Package

The complete Series AA package includes six key documents:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Term Sheet">
    ### Series AA Term Sheet
    
    **Non-binding** summary of key economic and governance terms for the financing.
    
    #### Key Sections
    
    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Economic Terms">
        - **Amount Raised**: Total size of financing round
        - **Pre-Money Valuation**: Company value before new investment
        - **Price Per Share**: Calculated from valuation and shares outstanding
        - **Liquidation Preference**: 1x non-participating (standard)
        - **Dividends**: Typically none for Series AA
      </Accordion>
      
      <Accordion title="Conversion Rights">
        - **Automatic Conversion**: Upon IPO or qualified financing
        - **Optional Conversion**: At holder's discretion
        - **Conversion Rate**: Initially 1:1 preferred to common
        - **Anti-Dilution**: Broad-based weighted average protection
      </Accordion>
      
      <Accordion title="Governance">
        - **Board Composition**: Typically 3-5 members
        - **Protective Provisions**: Key decisions requiring investor approval
        - **Information Rights**: Regular financial reporting
        - **Pro Rata Rights**: Ability to maintain ownership in future rounds
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
    
    #### What Makes It Founder-Friendly
    
    - 1x liquidation preference (not 2x or 3x)
    - Non-participating preferred (no "double dipping")
    - Broad-based weighted average anti-dilution (less dilutive)
    - Minimal protective provisions

    - [Docracy Version](https://www.docracy.com/5/y-combinator-series-aa-termsheet)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Stock Purchase Agreement">
    ### Stock Purchase Agreement (SPA)
    
    **Binding** contract governing the actual sale and purchase of preferred stock.
    
    #### Key Provisions
    
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Purchase & Sale">
        Investor agrees to purchase shares at specified price. Company agrees to sell.
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Closing Conditions">
        Conditions that must be satisfied before transaction closes:
        - Board approval
        - Stockholder approval
        - No material adverse changes
        - Legal opinions delivered
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Representations & Warranties">
        Company makes statements about:
        - Legal organization and authority
        - Capitalization and ownership
        - Financial condition
        - Intellectual property
        - Compliance with laws
        - No undisclosed liabilities
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Covenants">
        Company agrees to:
        - Maintain corporate existence
        - Provide financial information
        - Obtain necessary consents
        - Use proceeds for business purposes
      </Step>
    </Steps>
    
    #### Notable Features
    
    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Simplified Reps" icon="check">
        Streamlined representations vs. full VC documents
      </Card>
      <Card title="Reasonable Conditions" icon="balance-scale">
        Standard closing conditions without excessive requirements
      </Card>
      <Card title="Limited Indemnification" icon="shield">
        Focused on material breaches only
      </Card>
      <Card title="Standard Escrow" icon="vault">
        Typically no holdback or escrow required
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
    
    #### Download
    
    - [Series AA Stock Purchase Agreement (.docx)](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/Series_AA_SPA.docx)
    - [Official YC Series AA Page](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Certificate of Incorporation">
    ### Amended & Restated Certificate of Incorporation
    
    **Charter document** that creates the Series AA Preferred Stock and defines its rights.
    
    #### Creates New Stock Class
    
    The amended certificate adds Series AA Preferred Stock with specific rights:
    
    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Liquidation Rights">
        **Liquidation Preference: 1x Original Purchase Price**
        
        In a liquidation event (acquisition, dissolution):
        
        1. Series AA holders receive 1x their investment first
        2. After Series AA is paid, remaining proceeds go to common
        3. Non-participating (Series AA doesn't get additional share)
        
        Example:
        ```
        Company sells for $10M
        Series AA invested: $2M
        
        Distribution:
        - Series AA: $2M (1x preference)
        - Common: $8M (remainder)
        
        If company sells for $1M:
        - Series AA: $1M (entire proceeds)
        - Common: $0
        ```
      </Accordion>
      
      <Accordion title="Voting Rights">
        - Vote alongside common stock on as-converted basis
        - Protective provisions require Series AA approval for:
          - Changing rights of Series AA
          - Creating new senior securities
          - Liquidation or sale of company
          - Amending certificate or bylaws
          - Increasing/decreasing board size
      </Accordion>
      
      <Accordion title="Conversion Rights">
        - **Optional Conversion**: Any time at holder's discretion
        - **Automatic Conversion**: Upon (a) IPO or (b) 2/3 vote of Series AA
        - **Conversion Rate**: 1:1, subject to anti-dilution adjustments
        - **Anti-Dilution**: Broad-based weighted average formula
      </Accordion>
      
      <Accordion title="Dividend Rights">
        - **If Declared**: Series AA participates with common on as-converted basis
        - **Typically**: No dividends paid at seed stage
        - **Priority**: If dividends paid, Series AA gets same as common would receive
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
    
    #### Download
    
    - [Series AA Certificate of Incorporation (.docx)](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/Series_AA_COI.docx)
    - [Official YC Series AA Page](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Investors' Rights Agreement">
    ### Investors' Rights Agreement (IRA)
    
    **Ongoing agreement** between company and investors defining post-closing rights and obligations.
    
    #### Key Rights Granted
    
    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Information Rights" icon="file-chart-column">
        **Annual & Quarterly Financial Statements**
        
        Company must provide:
        - Annual audited financials within 120 days of year-end
        - Quarterly unaudited financials within 45 days of quarter-end
        - Annual budget and operating plan
        
        <Note>For major investors only (typically $100K+ investment)</Note>
      </Card>
      
      <Card title="Pro Rata Rights" icon="arrows-rotate">
        **Right to Participate in Future Rounds**
        
        Investors can maintain ownership percentage by:
        - Investing in proportion to existing ownership
        - Applying to all future equity financings
        - Not an obligation, just a right
        
        <Note>Typically for investors with 1%+ ownership</Note>
      </Card>
      
      <Card title="Registration Rights" icon="clipboard-list">
        **Future IPO Participation**
        
        If company goes public:
        - Demand rights (can force registration)
        - Piggyback rights (join company registration)
        - S-3 rights (simplified registration)
        
        <Note>Rarely exercised at seed stage but important for future</Note>
      </Card>
      
      <Card title="Right of First Refusal" icon="hand">
        **On Founder Stock Sales**
        
        Before founders can sell to third parties:
        - Company gets first right to buy
        - Then investors get right to buy
        - Prevents unwanted third parties
        
        <Note>Protects cap table composition</Note>
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
    
    #### Other Key Provisions
    
    - **Confidentiality**: Investors must keep information confidential
    - **Termination**: Rights expire upon IPO or sale
    - **Transferability**: Rights can transfer with shares (with conditions)
    - **Amendment**: Requires majority of investor consent
    
    #### Download
    
    - [Series AA Investors' Rights Agreement (.docx)](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/Series_AA_IRA.docx)
    - [Official YC Series AA Page](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Board Consent">
    ### Board Consent
    
    **Written action** by the Board of Directors approving the financing.
    
    #### What It Approves
    
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Financing Terms">
        Board approves the equity financing on terms in the Term Sheet
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Certificate Amendment">
        Board approves amended certificate creating Series AA Preferred
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Stock Issuance">
        Board authorizes issuance of Series AA shares to investors
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Transaction Documents">
        Board approves all transaction documents (SPA, IRA, etc.)
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Officer Authority">
        Board authorizes officers to execute documents and close transaction
      </Step>
    </Steps>
    
    #### Process
    
    <Note>
    Board Consent is typically executed via written consent in lieu of a meeting, allowing faster execution without scheduling a formal board meeting.
    </Note>
    
    #### Required Signatures
    
    - All directors must sign
    - If any director disagrees, must hold formal meeting with vote
    - Dissenting directors can abstain or vote against in formal meeting
    
    #### Download
    
    - [Series AA Board Consent (.docx)](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/Series_AA_Board_Consent.docx)
    - [Official YC Series AA Page](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Stockholder Consent">
    ### Stockholder Consent
    
    **Written action** by stockholders approving the financing and certificate amendment.
    
    #### What It Approves
    
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Certificate Amendment">
        Stockholders approve amended certificate of incorporation creating Series AA Preferred Stock
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Stock Issuance Authorization">
        Stockholders approve issuance of Series AA shares to investors
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Increasing Authorized Shares">
        If needed, stockholders approve increase in authorized shares
      </Step>
    </Steps>
    
    #### Who Must Sign?
    
    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Required Majority" icon="users">
        Typically requires approval by holders of majority of:
        - Common stock voting separately
        - All classes voting together
      </Card>
      
      <Card title="Founder Action" icon="pen">
        In practice at seed stage:
        - Founders typically own majority
        - Can execute consent immediately
        - No need for full stockholder meeting
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
    
    #### Process
    
    <Note>
    Like Board Consent, Stockholder Consent uses written consent in lieu of meeting for speed and efficiency. Delaware law allows this if permitted by certificate.
    </Note>
    
    #### Timing
    
    - Must be obtained before or concurrent with closing
    - Effective immediately upon execution by required majority
    - Filed with Delaware within required timeframe
    
    #### Download
    
    - [Series AA Stockholder Consent (.docx)](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/Series_AA_Stockholder_Consent.docx)
    - [Official YC Series AA Page](https://old.ycombinator.com/seriesaa/)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Comparison: Series AA vs. Alternatives

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="vs. SAFE">
    ### Series AA vs. SAFE
    
    | Aspect | Series AA | SAFE |
    |--------|-----------|------|
    | **Timing** | Immediate equity | Future equity |
    | **Valuation** | Set now | Set at future round |
    | **Ownership** | Clear from day one | Uncertain until conversion |
    | **Complexity** | Moderate (6 docs) | Simple (1 doc) |
    | **Cost** | Higher legal fees | Minimal legal fees |
    | **Investor Rights** | Immediate board/info rights | None until conversion |
    | **Best For** | Established startups | Very early stage |
    
    <Check>**Use SAFE when**: Pre-product, can't set valuation, need to move fast</Check>
    <Check>**Use Series AA when**: Post-product, clear valuation, ready for equity investors</Check>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="vs. Series Seed">
    ### Series AA vs. Series Seed
    
    | Aspect | Series AA | Series Seed |
    |--------|-----------|-------------|
    | **Origin** | Y Combinator | Cooley LLP |
    | **Complexity** | Lighter | More comprehensive |
    | **Protective Provisions** | Fewer | More standard VCs |
    | **Documentation** | 6 documents | 7+ documents |
    | **Philosophy** | Trust founders | Standard protections |
    | **Best For** | YC/angel rounds | Institutional seed |
    
    <Note>
    Series Seed is more widely adopted and may be preferred by institutional seed funds. Series AA is lighter and better for angel-dominated rounds.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="vs. NVCA Series A">
    ### Series AA vs. NVCA Series A
    
    | Aspect | Series AA | NVCA Series A |
    |--------|-----------|---------------|
    | **Target Round** | $250K-$2M seed | $2M+ institutional |
    | **Investors** | Angels, small funds | Venture capital firms |
    | **Complexity** | Simplified | Comprehensive |
    | **Documents** | 6 core docs | 10+ documents |
    | **Terms** | Founder-friendly | VC-standard |
    | **Board Control** | Founder maintains | Often splits control |
    | **Legal Costs** | $5K-$15K | $25K-$50K+ |
    
    <Warning>
    Don't use NVCA Series A documents for seed rounds - they're overly complex and expensive for early-stage deals.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Closing Process

Typical timeline and steps for a Series AA financing:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Negotiate & Sign Term Sheet">
    **Timeline: 1-2 weeks**
    
    - Agree on key terms with lead investor
    - Execute non-binding term sheet
    - Term sheet is starting point for documents
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Prepare Documents">
    **Timeline: 1-2 weeks**
    
    - Attorney drafts documents based on term sheet
    - Customize YC templates for your specific terms
    - Prepare cap table showing post-financing ownership
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Due Diligence">
    **Timeline: 1-2 weeks (concurrent with docs)**
    
    - Investors review company information
    - Legal review of corporate documents
    - Financial review of statements
    - IP review (if applicable)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Document Review & Negotiation">
    **Timeline: 1 week**
    
    - Investors review draft documents
    - Negotiate any changes (typically minimal)
    - Finalize all documents
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Obtain Approvals">
    **Timeline: 1-3 days**
    
    - Execute Board Consent
    - Execute Stockholder Consent
    - Both typically via DocuSign or similar
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Closing">
    **Timeline: 1 day**
    
    - All parties sign documents
    - Wire transfer of funds
    - File amended certificate with Delaware
    - Issue stock certificates
    - Update cap table
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
**Total Timeline**: Typically 4-6 weeks from term sheet to closing for a Series AA round. This is significantly faster than traditional VC rounds which can take 2-3 months.
</Note>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Choose the Right Time">
    Don't raise Series AA too early. Best after:
    - You've launched product
    - You have some revenue or clear path to revenue
    - You can justify a reasonable valuation
    - You've converted existing SAFEs or notes
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Set Realistic Valuation">
    Series AA requires setting a valuation. Make sure it's:
    - Justified by traction and metrics
    - Leaves room to grow into next round
    - Allows you to raise sufficient capital
    - Not so high that you can't raise Series A
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Clean Up Cap Table First">
    Before Series AA:
    - Convert all SAFEs and convertible notes
    - Buy out or convert any old option holders
    - Ensure all stock grants are properly documented
    - Have clean option pool ready
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Negotiate Minimally">
    YC documents are already founder-friendly:
    - Don't try to make them even lighter
    - Accept standard terms to close quickly
    - Save negotiating energy for larger rounds
    - Speed is more valuable than perfect terms
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Work with Startup Counsel">
    Even with standard docs, you need a lawyer:
    - Review all documents before signing
    - Customize schedules and exhibits
    - Ensure compliance with securities laws
    - File necessary documents with state
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is Series AA right for my company?">
    Series AA is best if:
    - You're raising $250K-$2M from angels
    - You have clear traction and can set valuation
    - You're past the SAFE stage
    - You want simple, founder-friendly terms
    
    Consider alternatives if:
    - You're pre-product or can't justify valuation (use SAFE)
    - You're raising from institutional VCs (use NVCA or Series Seed)
    - You're raising under $250K (use SAFEs)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I modify the documents?">
    Yes, but:
    - Documents are meant to be starting templates
    - Customize financial terms (amount, valuation, etc.)
    - Generally avoid modifying standard legal provisions
    - Work with attorney to ensure modifications are appropriate
    - Over-customization defeats purpose of standardization
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What about international companies?">
    YC documents are for Delaware corporations:
    - Not suitable for non-US companies
    - Need to adapt significantly for other jurisdictions
    - Consider YC-style principles but local documents
    - Work with local counsel familiar with startup financings
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How much will legal fees be?">
    Typical range:
    - **With standard YC docs**: $5,000 - $15,000
    - **With significant customization**: $15,000 - $25,000
    - **Depends on**: Round size, number of investors, complexity
    - **Save by**: Using standard terms, organized documents, decisive decision-making
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
