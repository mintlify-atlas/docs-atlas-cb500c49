## What is Series Seed?

Series Seed is a standardized set of legal documents that can be quickly and easily deployed for a seed investment round. This collection was developed by Cooley LLP and represents a streamlined approach to seed financing that balances the interests of both entrepreneurs and investors.

<Note>
  The Series Seed documents are designed for seed rounds typically ranging from $250K to $2M, though they can be adapted for different amounts based on your specific needs.
</Note>

## Key Features

### Simplicity and Speed
The Series Seed documents are intentionally straightforward, avoiding unnecessary complexity that can slow down early-stage financings. They provide essential protections while minimizing negotiation time.

### Balanced Terms
These documents reflect "market" terms that are generally fair to both founders and investors, incorporating lessons learned from thousands of seed financings.

## Document Sets Available

Series Seed includes two primary financing structures:

<Accordion title="Equity Financing Documents">
  Used when investors purchase preferred stock directly:
  
  - Term Sheet (Equity)
  - Preferred Stock Investment Agreement
  - Restated Certificate of Incorporation
  - Board Consent
  - Stockholder Consent
  - Investor Questionnaire
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Convertible Note Documents">
  Used when investors provide debt that converts to equity later:
  
  - Term Sheet (Convertible Note)
  - Convertible Promissory Note
  - Board Consent
  - Investor Questionnaire
</Accordion>

## When to Use Series Seed

Consider using Series Seed documents when:

- Raising your first institutional capital (seed round)
- Seeking a balance between founder-friendly and investor-protective terms
- Working with professional angel investors or seed funds
- Looking to close quickly without extensive legal negotiations
- Raising between $250K and $2M (though adaptable)

<Warning>
  While Series Seed documents are designed to be straightforward, they still create legally binding obligations. Always consult with qualified legal counsel before using these documents.
</Warning>

## Comparison with Other Options

### Series Seed vs. SAFE
While Y Combinator's SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) is simpler and faster, Series Seed equity documents provide more structure and immediate investor rights, which some investors prefer.

### Series Seed vs. Full Series A
Series Seed documents are lighter than typical Series A documents. They have fewer protective provisions, simpler governance structures, and less extensive representations and warranties.

## Key Terms Overview

<Accordion title="Liquidation Preference">
  Series Seed typically includes 1x non-participating liquidation preference, meaning preferred stockholders receive their investment back before common stockholders in a liquidation event.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Board Composition">
  Board structure is negotiable but typically includes founder representatives and may include one or more investor representatives depending on the round size.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Information Rights">
  Major Purchasers (those investing above a specified threshold) receive annual and quarterly financial statements and inspection rights.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Participation Rights">
  Major Purchasers have pro rata rights to participate in future financing rounds to maintain their ownership percentage.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Drag-Along Rights">
  If approved by required majorities, all stockholders must participate in a sale of the company on the same terms.
</Accordion>

## Getting Started

1. **Determine your financing structure**: Equity vs. convertible note
2. **Review the term sheet**: Understand key economic and governance terms
3. **Consult with counsel**: Have experienced startup counsel review terms
4. **Generate documents**: Use the automated tools with your specific terms
5. **Execute and close**: Sign documents and receive funding

<Note>
  The Series Seed documents are designed to work together as a complete package. Modifying individual documents without considering the entire set may create inconsistencies.
</Note>
