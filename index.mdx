  <Steps>
    <Step title="Choose your formation structure">
      Decide whether to form a corporation or LLC. Most venture-backed startups incorporate in Delaware as a C-corp, which provides the most flexibility for future fundraising. LLCs work well for smaller businesses or those seeking pass-through taxation.
    </Step>
    <Step title="File formation documents">
      Use the certificate of incorporation (for corporations) or operating agreement (for LLCs) templates to establish your legal entity. These documents define ownership structure, stock classes, and governance rules.
      
      ```bash
      # Example: Delaware Corporation Filing
      # File Certificate of Incorporation with DE Secretary of State
      # Initial filing fee: $89 + $50 for certified copy
      ```
    </Step>
    <Step title="Execute founder agreements">
      Sign a Founder Accord to memorialize equity splits, vesting schedules, and each founder's role. This prevents disputes and establishes clear expectations from day one.
      
      <Accordion title="What should a Founder Accord include?">
        A Founder Accord should cover equity allocation, vesting terms (typically 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff), roles and responsibilities, time commitment, IP assignment, and conditions for departure. See the [Founder Accord](/founders/founder-accord) guide for details.
      </Accordion>
    </Step>
  </Steps>
