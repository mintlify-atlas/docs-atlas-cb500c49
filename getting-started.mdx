## Deploying documents via OpenLaw

OpenLaw is a protocol for creating and executing legal agreements. Many documents in the Startup Starter Pack are available as OpenLaw templates for fast, automated deployment.

### What is OpenLaw?

OpenLaw provides:

- **Smart contract integration** - Legal agreements that can interact with blockchain technology
- **Automated execution** - Fill in variables once and generate complete documents
- **Digital signatures** - Built-in e-signature functionality
- **Version control** - Track changes and maintain document history
- **Template marketplace** - Access to community-created legal templates

### How to deploy an OpenLaw template

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access the template">
    Click on any "OpenLaw Template" link in the documentation. This will take you directly to the template in the OpenLaw library.
    
    Example: [Founder Accord OpenLaw Template](https://app.openlaw.io/template/Founder%20Accord)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create an OpenLaw account">
    If you don't have an account, sign up at [OpenLaw.io](https://openlaw.io). The basic account is free for individual use.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill in the variables">
    OpenLaw templates use smart variables. Fill in information like:
    - Party names and addresses
    - Equity percentages
    - Vesting schedules
    - Financial terms
    
    The template will automatically populate these values throughout the document.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review the generated document">
    OpenLaw will generate a complete legal document based on your inputs. Review it carefully to ensure all information is correct.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Execute and sign">
    Use OpenLaw's built-in signature functionality to execute the agreement. You can send it to other parties for electronic signature directly through the platform.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Some OpenLaw templates include optional integrations like Gnosis multisig wallet deployment or automatic LLC filing services. These are clearly marked in the template interface.
</Note>

## Finding the right documents

Use this decision tree to determine which documents you need:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Starting to work with co-founders on an idea">
    **You need:** [Founder Accord](/founders/founder-accord)
    
    This simple agreement establishes the basic structure for collaboration before you officially form a company. It covers equity splits, roles, and time commitments.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Ready to form a legal entity">
    **You need:** Formation documents
    
    Choose between:
    - [LLC Formation](/formation/llc-formation) - Simpler structure, pass-through taxation, more flexibility
    - [Corporation Formation](/formation/incorporation) - Better for venture capital fundraising, preferred by most investors
    
    Delaware is the most common jurisdiction for both LLCs and corporations.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Bringing on advisors">
    **You need:** [Advisor agreements](/business/advisor-agreements)
    
    Use the **Founder Advisor Standard Template (FAST)** for standardized equity compensation, or a general **Advisor Agreement** for more flexibility in compensation structure.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Raising seed capital">
    **You need:** Seed financing documents
    
    Options include:
    - [Y Combinator SAFE](/financing/safe-agreements) - Fastest and simplest, no debt or equity until a future financing
    - [Convertible Note](/financing/convertible-notes) - Bridge financing that converts to equity later
    - [Series Seed Equity](/financing/term-sheets) - Full preferred stock financing with investor rights
    - [Shared Earnings Agreement](/financing/shared-earnings-agreement) - Revenue-based alternative to equity financing
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Launching a website or product">
    **You need:** Website documents - [Terms of Use](/business/terms-of-use) and [Privacy Policy](/business/privacy-policy)
    
    Includes:
    - **Terms of Use** - Governs how users can interact with your website/product
    - **Privacy Policy** - Required by law in most jurisdictions, explains how you handle user data
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Sharing confidential information">
    **You need:** [Mutual NDA](/business/mutual-nda)
    
    Use this when discussing your idea or technology with potential partners, investors, or service providers who need access to proprietary information.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Comparing different sources

The Library section includes documents from multiple sources for the same legal need. This allows you to:

- **Compare approaches** - See how Y Combinator, NVCA, and Techstars handle the same issues
- **Understand market terms** - Identify what's standard across different documents
- **Choose the best fit** - Select documents that match your stage, industry, and goals

### Key differences between sources

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Y Combinator" icon="y">
    Most founder-friendly terms, optimized for speed and simplicity. Best for very early-stage companies.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Series Seed" icon="seedling">
    Balanced terms for seed rounds, widely accepted by both founders and investors. Good middle ground.
  </Card>
  <Card title="NVCA" icon="building">
    Most comprehensive and investor-friendly. Industry standard for Series A and later rounds.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Techstars" icon="star">
    Designed for $250k-$2M seed rounds, leans slightly toward founders but includes important investor protections.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  While these documents come from reputable sources, every startup situation is unique. Always have an attorney review documents before execution, especially for significant transactions like fundraising or major partnerships.
</Warning>
